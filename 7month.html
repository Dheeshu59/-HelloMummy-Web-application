  7 Month Pregnant: Symptoms and Fetal Development.


  Dearest Mother, welcome to week seven of your pregnancy!
  This week will bring a lot of changes for you,
  as your child continues to grow at an alarming rate.
  Your embryo is around the size of a small blueberry at 7 weeks pregnant,
  measuring about 0.3 inches in diameter.
  Continue reading to learn more about how things are going in your belly this week.




 
Symptoms of Pregnancy at Seven Month Pregnant

Although some of the symptoms from the previous weeks are still present, here's what you might be feeling:


  Excess salivation

  While drooling may seem strange, it is simply another sign of pregnancy.
  More saliva in your mouth than usual is a common symptom of morning sickness.


  Food cravings or aversions

Hormones may cause changes in your food preferences and tolerance for aromas/odors
  throughout the seventh week of pregnancy. It's quite natural
  if you can't handle the smell of a food you used to enjoy before you became pregnant.
  For further information, consult with your doctor.


  Nausea

 At this point, morning sickness may be in full force, making you feel horrible. But, hey, it'll only be a few weeks before it goes away completely during the second trimester. Increased blood volume and hormones cause the body to produce surplus fluid, so you may find yourself going to the bathroom more frequently. However, don't allow this discomfort deter you from remaining hydrated; you'll need to drink enough of water during your pregnancy.


  Diarrhoea

 - Your diarrhoea could be a gastrointestinal ailment like constipation or indigestion at this point. Hormone fluctuations could be the cause of your symptoms. Applesauce, oats, and bananas should all be included in your diet.


 Cramping

 As your uterus swells, you can feel some discomfort in the form of moderate cramps, which is quite normal. However, if the pain gets severe or lasts for an extended period of time, see your doctor right away.


 Fatigue

  As your progesterone levels rise quickly, you may feel sleepy and exhausted. Rest as much as possible to take care of yourself



  Fetal Development

 
 During the seventh week of pregnancy, an ultrasound is performed. Major development is taking on inside your belly, which you can see during your 7-week pregnancy ultrasound. You'll see that important organs like the liver, pancreas, and appendix have formed in the fetus. The development of his or her ears, eyes, nose, mouth, fingers, and toes has occurred. Brainy baby Even while brain growth continues after birth, the basic brain structure has already been established. Your baby's brain will divide into three parts in the next weeks: forebrain, midbrain, and hindbrain. The mid-brain distributes electrical signals to the appropriate destinations throughout the brain, whereas the fore-brain is important for developing and retaining memories, reasoning, and problem-solving. Breathing, heart rate, and muscle movement are all controlled by the hindbrain.
  The arms and legs of the baby begin to develop.
  Arms, shoulder segments, hands, legs, knees, and foot segments are growing from your baby's limb buds, which will become stronger and longer as they divide into arms, shoulder segments, hands, legs, knees, and foot segments.
  The kidneys have been implanted.
  Your baby's kidneys will now begin to function and manage waste as he or she begins to produce pee.
  What's more, it's in the works.
  The lips, nostrils, ears, and eyes of your baby are becoming more distinct. The eyelids and tongue of your baby are also beginning to form. While connecting the baby to the placenta, the umbilical cord has been forming. It is in charge of transporting oxygenated blood and nutrients to your baby as well as removing waste. In the womb, babies go through three stages of kidney development, and your baby is most likely in the second.
