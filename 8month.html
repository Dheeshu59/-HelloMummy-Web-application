  8 Month Pregnant: Symptoms and Fetal Development.



  Hello, future mother!
  You're about to finish your second month of pregnancy.
  In terms of development, your baby has come a long way.
  Your baby's growth will pick up speed soon,
  and you'll be holding your bundle of joy in no time.
  Continue reading to learn more about your eight weeks.





  Symptoms of Pregnancy at Eight Month Pregnant

  Your symptoms will be in full swing at 8 weeks pregnant.
  But keep in mind that these signs and symptoms aren't always consistent.
  Other expectant mothers' symptoms may not be the same as yours.
  For the time being, you may not be experiencing any of these symptoms,
  or you may be experiencing none at all, and they may appear the following week.
  Here's what you might encounter:


 Morning sickness is a common occurrence.

  Nausea and vomiting may be bothering you right now, but by the second trimester,
  these symptoms will be gone. You're almost there,
  so relax! When you first wake up, munch on some crackers,
 and then have six smaller meals throughout the day.


 Aversions to certain foods and smells

 Some foods and scents that previously didn't bother you may suddenly bother you. When you see certain meals or smell certain odors, you may feel nauseated. This is natural and occurs as a result of the elevated amounts of pregnant hormones. Don't worry; your appetite will return soon.


  Diarrhoea

 Because your digestive system will be more sensitive today, eat healthful foods and remain hydrated. Consult your doctor if you have diarrhoea for more than a day or if you have any other symptoms.


 Urination on a regular basis

 Are you still making extra trips to the restroom? It's all right! This is a symptom that you will have throughout your pregnancy. As your uterus swells, it puts pressure on your bladder, causing you to feel the need to go to the bathroom.


  Abdominal cramps

  Period cramps might be caused by your uterus expanding. However, if the cramps become unbearable, consult your doctor to rule out any serious issues.


 Back ache

  To accommodate your uterus, your back muscles have to work harder, which might cause pain in your lower back.


  Fatigue

 Do you feel drained? Increased progesterone levels are to blame! When you have the opportunity, take a few additional naps. Pay attention to your body's requirements and relax.


  Sleeping problems

 Discomfort, frequent trips to the bathroom, and fluctuating hormone levels can make getting a decent night's sleep extremely difficult. Before retiring to bed, listen to soothing music, sip warm milk, or take a shower or a hot water bath. It may be beneficial to lie on your left side because it improves blood circulation.



  Fetal Development

 
 You're in your second month of pregnancy, and there's a lot going on within your womb. Your baby has grown to be around 0.5 to 0.6 inches tall and the size of a raspberry at this point. Here's a look at what's going on with embryo development this week: With the formation of eyelids, noses, and lips, your kid now resembles a human. Her hands, fingers, feet, and toes will have developed The heart of your baby beats between 150 and 170 times per minute. The small trunk and limbs of your baby are now twitching and making voluntary motions. His digestive system and intestines are also developing, but they are forced to go into the umbilical cord due to a shortage of space inside the embryo. The ears of your baby begin to develop outside of her head, and her reproductive organs develop into ovaries or testes.
