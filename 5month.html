  5 Month Pregnant: Symptoms and Fetal Development.



  Welcome to the most wonderful month of your pregnancy!
  This month will bring you a cute baby bump, a burst of energy,
  your first baby kicks, an ultrasound photo to preserve,
  and that much-discussed rosy pregnant glow. You still don't believe us?
  Take a look just at list below to learn more.





  Symptoms of Pregnancy at Five Month Pregnant

  Although you may still be experiencing the energy surge that is common in the second trimester,
  you may also be experiencing some bothersome symptoms such as:


  Feet swollen

  Due to the pregnant hormone relaxin, fluid retention, and pregnancy weight increase,
  your feet may enlarge. The muscles in your body and the joints in your feet loosen up as you
  prepare for childbirth by relaxing and expanding your feet. To aid with swelling,
  soak your feet in a cool bath. You can also use a pillow to elevate your feet.


  Back pain in the lower back

  When you're five months pregnant, your posture may start to change as your uterus expands.
  Your center of gravity will shift as your hump grows larger. Your lower back may be strained as
  your muscles work overtime to support your increased weight and changing body form.
  Exercises to strengthen your back muscles should be done regularly,
  and you should sit in seats with appropriate back support or a pillow behind your back.
  Consult your doctor if you are experiencing too much pain.


  Dizziness

  A change in your blood circulation during pregnancy can cause reduced blood flow to your head, resulting in a dizzy feeling. It can happen if you suddenly get up or change positions.
  Take it slowly and cautiously. When you're dizzy, don't stand up.


  Nasal congestion

  Pregnancy hormones can cause the mucus membranes in your nasal passages to dry out, resulting in symptoms such as nosebleeds, stuffy noses, and runny noses.
  To ease congestion, use saline drops or a humidifier.


 Sleeping problems

  Due to a larger hump, you're having trouble finding a comfortable sleeping position. Sleeping on your side with a pillow between your knees is a  good option. For further support, place a cushion under your stomach. Before going to bed, do some light workouts like walking, swimming, or  prenatal yoga. Taking a warm, relaxing bath before bedtime can also help you sleep better.



  Fetal Development

 
  Yay! Month by month, your tiny marvel becomes more active. You'll be feeling the kicks and flips in no time.  Your baby will now start sleeping and waking up at predictable times. Outside noises may startle her,
  so don't be shocked if you experience a reaction after hearing something loud.
  Your baby's sucking reflex will begin to activate around the end of the fifth month, preparing him or her for feeding after birth.
  In the womb, your 5-month-old kid develops vernix caseosa, a slippery, greasy layer that protects his skin in the amniotic sac.
  After your baby is born, this vernix will cover her entire body.
  Lanugo, a fine, velvety hair that maintains the vernix in place on the skin, will also form in your infant.The majority of this lanugo will vanish after  your kid is born. Even after birth, some babies have little patches of lanugo on various portions of their bodies.
