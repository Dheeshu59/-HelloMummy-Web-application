  Symptoms of Pregnancy at Three Month Pregnant

  You may still be experiencing some of the early pregnancy symptoms at three months pregnant,
  but you may also notice some new ones. Some of these symptoms may be difficult to deal with;
  bear in mind that you may not experience all of them.


 Increase in vaginal discharge

  A combination of pregnant hormones and your body's increased blood supply can cause a bit more
  vaginal discharge than usual. It's probably nothing to be concerned about as long as
  it's clear or whitish and doesn't smell awful. To help avoid vaginal infections, use cotton underwear
  and loose, breathable clothing. If you're concerned about what's going on, speak with your doctor.


 Nausea

  You may still be feeling nauseous, but only for a short while. Many expectant mothers report that
  their morning sickness begins to fade during this month, which is fantastic news!
  If you're not feeling well, eat bland things like toast, rice, or bananas and drink ginger ale
  or ginger tea to help your stomach feel better.


  Fatigue

  As your body continues to nourish your baby, you may feel sleepy this month. Rest when you can, stay hydrated, and engage in some light activity to help you sleep better. Prenatal yoga,  walking, and swimming are all healthy options, but talk to your doctor before starting anything new.


  Skin changes

  If you've observed that the color of your nipples has darkened, this is due to your body manufacturing more melanin, a pigment. Chloasma is a term for brown areas on the face caused  by excess melanin. A black, vertical line running from your belly button to your pubic area may also be visible. As your tummy grows larger, this line may begin to develop at three  months pregnant. After your baby is born, the majority of these discolorations will fade or disappear.


 Breast changes

  This month, your breasts may also expand and change. Your areolas may become larger and darker, and your nipples may protrude somewhat. Milk  glands are ready to make milk beneath the surface, and fat is being added to your breasts. It's probably time to go up a size if your bras are too tight.  To get a new bra that is more comfortable, go to your local department or specialized lingerie store for a professional bra fitting.


 Constipation

  Some pregnancy hormones can induce constipation by slowing down your digestive tract. It's also possible that the additional iron in your prenatal  supplements is to blame. Make sure to drink enough of water and eat plenty of fiber. Fiber is abundant in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.



  Fetal Development

 The intestines and musculoskeletal system of your baby are developing on the inside.
  Although some bones may begin to solidify, the backbone remains soft. On the exterior,
  your baby's hands and feet are developing small fingers and toes, and by three months pregnant,
  they may even have the beginnings of fingernails and toenails.
  Your baby's external genitals will begin to form at some point this month,
  and you'll know whether you're having a girl or a boy before you know it!
  Keep in mind that you'll find out whether you're having a boy or a girl during your mid-pregnancy ultrasound,
  but if you want to have some fun now, take our gender quiz.
