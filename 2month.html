  2 Month Pregnant: Symptoms and Fetal Development.



  You can be feeling overwhelmed, delighted, scared, happy,
  or all of the above at two months pregnant.
  That's what you get when you're pregnant!
  There's little doubt you'll be thinking a lot this month.
  Continue reading to learn more about common pregnancy symptoms,
  how your baby will develop in the coming weeks,
  and what else you may expect at two months pregnant.





  Symptoms of Pregnancy at two Month Pregnant

  Some of the most frequent pregnancy symptoms at two months pregnant include:


  Morning sickness

  Between weeks four and nine of pregnancy, nausea and vomiting are common. Morning sickness
  can strike at any time of day, not just in the morning, contrary to common perception. Maintaining
  a balanced diet, avoiding oily and spicy foods, and eating smaller, more frequent meals
  may be beneficial. To assist regulate your blood sugar levels, eat some plain crackers
  first thing in the morning before getting out of bed. To settle your stomach,rest, stay hydrated,
  and drink ginger ale or ginger tea.Morning sickness isn't something that all expecting mothers
  experience,but if you do,know that it usually goes away by the second trimester. If your symptoms
  are especially severe, consult a doctor.


  Mood changes

  Be prepared to be a little more emotional than usual. Your body is experiencing a hormonal surge,
  which can cause some dramatic mood swings. Your moods may also change as a result of how you're physically
  or psychologically feeling. It's common to feel down from time to time if you experience morning sickness and
  it's making you feel uncomfortable and stressed.


 Food aversions

 Certain foods, or even fragrances, that you used to enjoy may become unpleasant or even cause nausea now that you're expecting. It's fine to stick to blander foods till your appetite  returns if your tastes have changed (usually in the second trimester). Just make sure to talk to your doctor about how to maintain a balanced diet if certain items are temporarily off the  table. You can also get some helpful hints by downloading our pregnancy nutrition guide.


  Heartburn and Indigestion

 The valve that links your stomach and esophagus can also relax as a result of pregnancy hormones. Stomach acid can flow into the esophagus when this happens, giving this unpleasant  symptom. If you have heartburn, stay away from spicy or fried foods.


  Constipation

 A feeling of being blocked up is a common early pregnancy sensation. Progesterone, a hormone that slows digestion, may cause constipation. If your  prenatal supplements contain a lot of iron, they can cause constipation. However, staying hydrated and active can help.


  Fatigue

 Because being pregnant requires a lot of energy, it's totally natural to feel exhausted or sleepier than usual. Rest when you can, even if it means  turning down a few requests. A balanced diet and regular exercise can help you feel more energized. Take solace in the knowledge that many  expectant mothers find their energy levels rise in the third trimester.



  Fetal Development

  Your baby begins to grow a tiny nose, mouth, and ears this month. The eyelids have formed,
  and they will remain fused shut until the second trimester.
  This month also marks the beginning of some significant organ development.
  The lungs of your child are now connected to the throat via breathing tubes.
  The heart, like the lungs, still has a lot of growth to do,
  but it's currently pounding at around 105 beats per minute - impressive!
  The amniotic sac has grown to its full size and is now filled with amniotic fluid.
  Its main responsibility is to house and safeguard your baby from now until birth.
  Not only that, but there's more! Another big milestone awaits you at the conclusion of week eight,
  or around the end of your second month:In medical terminology, your tiny one progresses from an embryo to a fetus.
